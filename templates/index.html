Face Detection

Face detection is a crucial biometric technique that analyzes unique facial features like eyes, nose, and jawline. It uses deep convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and transformers to capture fine-grained patterns. Our system uses ResNet50 for spatial encoding and ViT for contextual understanding. This dual-model approach increases accuracy, even under varied lighting or facial expressions. Face recognition ensures fast, contactless identification in real-world environments.

Iris Detection

Iris recognition captures the unique patterns in the colored part of the eye surrounding the pupil. It is one of the most stable and accurate biometric methods due to minimal variability over time. Our model uses wavelet-based texture analysis and ViT to deeply analyze iris features. This combination provides high resistance to spoofing and ensures reliable identification. Iris detection is widely used in border security, banking, and mobile authentication.

Fingerprint Detection

Fingerprint authentication uses the distinct ridges and valleys on human fingers to verify identity. We extract wavelet-transformed features and use a custom CNN model for classification. This method is non-intrusive and highly scalable for large population systems. Fingerprint recognition is commonly used in attendance, mobile access, and criminal databases. Our system ensures robust and quick fingerprint matching, even with partial inputs.
